Executive Summary

The goal of this project is to run a fama macbeth regression using annual financial data, specifically Earnings to price ratio, 1/price, price-normalized accruals, and market capitalization. All data was taken from the CRSP/Compustat Merged-Fundamentals data set.

Calculations for the Fama Macbeth Regression

WRDS was used to query the datasets required for these calculations. The stock universe included all stocks CRSP has within their database for all years. The earnings to price ratio was calculated by dividing earnings per share excluding extraordinary items divided by market value divided by number of shares outstanding. The price ratio was simply one divided by the annual closing price of the stock. Accruals are accrued revenue and accrued expenses divided by the market capitalization to acheive a price normalized ratio. The accrued revenue was empty through the entire WRDS database so only accrued expenses was used. Market cap is closing price multiplied by shares outstanding and then divided by 1 thousand to make market capitalization in the same units as the other factors. across all firms there ended up being twenty years of usable data to run the cross sectional regression from 1998-2017. To find market excess returns the annual value weighted returns from the S&P500 was subtracted from the annual returns for each stock. I ended with twenty lambdas that were averaged to get the 4 lambdas related to each factor. All lambdas were found to be very small with standard errors of similar magnitude. None of the factors used were shown to be very useful predictors of future market performance. The price ratio seemed to be most promising, having the highest lambda of about .004 with a standard error of about .0018 so it could possible provide excess market return between .22% to .58%.
